# Tactic 01: Entity Stacking & Brand Foundations

## What It Is
Entity stacking is the process of creating consistent brand presence across 20+ authoritative platforms to establish your business as a recognized "entity" in Google's Knowledge Graph. This includes social profiles, business directories, and Web 2.0 properties — all with consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) and brand information.

## Why It Works
Google's algorithm increasingly relies on entity recognition. The 2024 algorithm leak confirmed that Google builds entity profiles from cross-platform signals. When Google sees your brand consistently referenced across LinkedIn, Crunchbase, YouTube, Medium, etc., it begins to trust your site as a real, legitimate entity rather than a thin affiliate or spam site. This is the digital equivalent of "being a real business."

**Algorithm reasoning:** Google's Knowledge Graph connects entities across the web. More consistent signals = stronger entity confidence = faster trust building. Without entity stacking, aggressive link building looks unnatural to Google because there's no supporting entity footprint.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Week 1: Core Platforms (4 hours)
1. **Google Business Profile** — Even for non-local businesses. Verify with postcard or phone
2. **LinkedIn Company Page** — Complete all fields, add logo, banner, description
3. **Facebook Business Page** — With complete info, consistent branding
4. **X/Twitter** — Professional profile, link to website
5. **YouTube Channel** — Even if you don't plan to post videos yet
6. **Pinterest Business** — Good for visual niches
7. **Instagram Business** — Link in bio

### Week 2: Authority Platforms (3 hours)
8. **Crunchbase** — Free basic listing, great for SaaS/startups
9. **About.me** — Personal brand page
10. **Medium** — Publish at least 1 article linking back
11. **GitHub** — If relevant (especially for tech/SaaS)
12. **AngelList/Wellfound** — For startups
13. **Product Hunt** — Submit your product
14. **G2/Capterra** — For software companies

### Week 3: Schema & Verification (2 hours)
15. Add **Organization schema markup** to your website
16. Add **LocalBusiness schema** if applicable
17. Add **sameAs** properties linking to all profiles
18. Verify Google Knowledge Panel claim if available
19. Submit to **Wikidata** (helps Google's entity recognition)

## Hidden Tips & Tricks

- **Wikidata is the secret weapon**: Most SEOs skip this. Creating a Wikidata entry for your brand (if notable enough) significantly accelerates entity recognition. Google pulls directly from Wikidata for Knowledge Panels.
- **"sameAs" schema is crucial**: Add ALL your profile URLs in the sameAs property of your Organization schema. This explicitly tells Google "these are all me."
- **Profile completeness matters more than profile count**: A 100% completed LinkedIn page is worth more than 10 half-empty profiles. Google measures profile completeness.
- **Use the EXACT same brand name everywhere**: Even slight variations ("My Company" vs "My Company Inc." vs "MyCompany") confuse entity recognition. Pick one canonical name.
- **Don't forget Google's own properties**: Google Scholar profiles, Google Maps, Google Business Profile, YouTube — Google trusts its own platforms most.
- **Post at least once on each platform**: Empty profiles provide minimal entity signal. One quality post per platform dramatically increases the signal strength.
- **Image consistency**: Use the same logo and brand colors everywhere. Google's image recognition cross-references visual branding.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Inconsistent NAP**: Different addresses or phone numbers across platforms confuse entity signals
2. **Creating profiles and abandoning them**: Empty profiles can look spammy
3. **Rushing through all 20+ in one day**: Spread creation over 2-3 weeks to look natural
4. **Using exact-match keyword names instead of brand names**: Your entity should be your brand, not "Best SEO Tool 2026"
5. **Forgetting to add schema markup on the website itself**: Profiles without corresponding schema lose half their value
6. **Not claiming/verifying profiles**: Unverified profiles carry less weight
