# Tactic 04: Competitor Backlink Gap Analysis

## What It Is
Using tools to identify websites that link to your competitors but not to you, then systematically pursuing those same link opportunities. This is "reverse engineering" your competitors' link profiles to find proven, achievable link sources.

## Why It Works
If a site links to multiple competitors in your niche, they clearly link to sites like yours — making them far more likely to link to you too. This removes the guesswork from prospecting. Instead of hoping a random site will be interested, you're targeting sites with a proven pattern of linking to businesses in your space. The conversion rate is 2-3x higher than random outreach because relevance is pre-validated.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Identify Your Top 3-5 SERP Competitors
1. Use your SEO data source's competitor data tool to find organic search competitors
2. Don't just pick the biggest — pick competitors at similar or slightly higher authority
3. Include 1-2 aspirational competitors (DR 50+) and 1-2 realistic ones (similar DR to you)

### Step 2: Run Backlink Gap Analysis
1. Use backlink gap data with your domain vs top 3 competitors
2. Export the list of domains linking to competitors but not to you
3. Sort by: number of competitors they link to (2+ = highest priority)

### Step 3: Categorize Opportunities (2 hours)
Tag each prospect by link type:
- **Directory/listing** — Easy to replicate (just submit)
- **Resource page** — Medium difficulty (pitch your content)
- **Blog post/article** — Hard (need guest post or outreach)
- **News/PR** — Very hard (need digital PR angle)
- **Forum/community** — Easy (participate and share)

### Step 4: Prioritize and Outreach
1. Start with directories and listings (quick wins)
2. Move to resource pages (medium effort)
3. Guest post opportunities last (high effort but high value)
4. For each category, create tailored outreach

### Step 5: Monitor and Iterate Monthly
1. Re-run gap analysis monthly
2. New competitor backlinks = new opportunities
3. Track which prospect categories convert best

## Hidden Tips & Tricks

- **The "2+ competitor" filter is magic**: If a site links to 3 out of 3 competitors, they're almost certainly a resource page, directory, or industry blog that you can get listed on too. Prioritize these heavily.
- **Check the linking PAGE, not just the domain**: A DR 60 site linking from a spammy guest post is less valuable than a DR 30 site linking from a genuine editorial article. Always check the actual linking page.
- **Look at anchor text patterns**: If competitors got links with branded anchors from a site, it's probably a natural editorial link. If it's exact-match keyword anchors, it's probably paid/guest post — approach differently.
- **Time-based gap analysis**: Run new/lost backlink tracking on competitors to see their RECENT link acquisitions. Fresh links mean active link-building — those sites are currently open to linking.
- **The "me too" pitch works**: When reaching out to a site that links to competitors, saying "I noticed you recommend [competitor]. We offer [differentiator]" is 3x more effective than a generic pitch.
- **Don't replicate BAD links**: Some competitor backlinks are from PBNs or link farms. Use judgment to filter these out — replicating them hurts you.
- **Export competitor anchors**: The anchor text distribution of competitor backlinks tells you what Google considers natural for your niche. Mirror that distribution in your own link building.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Only analyzing the #1 competitor**: Smaller competitors often have more replicable link profiles
2. **Ignoring nofollow links in the gap**: Nofollow from DR 80+ sites (Wikipedia, Reddit, Quora) still have value
3. **Treating all gap opportunities equally**: A directory link takes 5 minutes; a guest post takes 5 hours
4. **Not filtering by relevance**: A competitor's link from an unrelated niche isn't worth pursuing
5. **Running the analysis once and never again**: Competitors build new links monthly — so should your analysis

## How Data Helps

- **competitor data**: Automatically identifies your true SERP competitors
- **backlink gap data**: Core tool — shows domains linking to up to 3 competitors but not you
- **new/lost backlink tracking**: Monitor competitor's new link acquisitions for fresh opportunities
- **opportunity detection**: Identify which of YOUR pages would benefit most from the links you find
