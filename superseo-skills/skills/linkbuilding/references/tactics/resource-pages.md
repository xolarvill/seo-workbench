# Tactic 09: Resource Page Link Building

## What It Is
Finding curated "resources," "useful links," or "recommended tools" pages in your niche and pitching your content for inclusion. These are pages specifically designed to link out to helpful resources — making them natural targets for link building.

## Why It Works
Resource page curators actively WANT to link out — their page's value comes from the quality of links they provide. This means you're not asking someone to go out of their way; you're helping them improve their existing resource. The intent is already aligned, making conversion rates higher than most other outreach tactics (5-15%).

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Find Resource Pages (2 hours)
Use Google search operators:
- `[niche] intitle:"resources" OR intitle:"useful links" OR inurl:resources`
- `[niche] "helpful resources" OR "recommended tools" OR "best tools"`
- `[niche] intitle:"links we love" OR intitle:"our favorite"`
- `[niche] inurl:links OR inurl:resources`

### Step 2: Qualify Prospects
- DR 20+ and real organic traffic
- Page is actively maintained (updated within past year)
- Links are relevant to your content
- Page has reasonable number of outbound links (not 500+)

### Step 3: Identify Your Best Content to Pitch
- Match your content to gaps in their resource page
- Your content should be the BEST resource on that specific topic
- Use opportunity detection to identify your strongest pages

### Step 4: Personalized Outreach
- Reference specific resources they already link to
- Explain why your content adds value their readers would appreciate
- Keep it brief (4-6 sentences)
- One CTA: "Would [your content] be a good fit for your [page name]?"

## Hidden Tips & Tricks

- **University resource pages are gold**: Search `site:.edu [niche] resources`. University departments maintain resource pages with DR 70-90+. They update less frequently, so less competition for placement.
- **Government resource pages exist too**: `site:.gov [niche] resources` — incredibly high authority and rarely targeted by SEOs.
- **The "reverse resource page" technique**: Create YOUR OWN resource page, link to potential partners generously, THEN reach out saying "I featured your work on my resource page. Would you consider adding our [specific content] to yours?"
- **Check the page's backlinks**: A resource page that itself has many backlinks passes more equity. Prioritize resource pages that rank for keywords.
- **Use backlink gap data**: Many backlink gap results ARE resource pages. This tool essentially does the prospecting for you.
- **Timing matters**: Submit before Q4 — many resource pages get updated annually in Q1. Getting in before the review cycle increases chances.
- **The "missing category" pitch**: If their resource page covers Topics A, B, and C but not D (which you cover), pitch as: "I noticed your page doesn't include [Topic D] resources yet. Here's one that might be a good addition."

## Common Mistakes

1. **Pitching mediocre content**: Your content must genuinely deserve to be on the page
2. **Not personalizing the pitch**: Generic "I found your resources page" gets deleted
3. **Targeting dead resource pages**: Check if the page has been updated recently
4. **Pitching your homepage instead of specific content**: Link to your most relevant, in-depth page
5. **Ignoring nofollow resource pages**: High-DR nofollow resource page links still have value
