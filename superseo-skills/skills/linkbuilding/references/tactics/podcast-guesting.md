# Tactic 15: Podcast Guesting for Backlinks 🆕

## What It Is
Appearing as a guest on podcasts in your niche to earn backlinks from episode show notes pages. Each podcast appearance typically generates 1-3 backlinks: the main podcast website, Apple Podcasts listing, and sometimes syndicated episode pages. Beyond links, it builds brand authority and drives referral traffic.

## Why It Works
Podcast hosts publish episode pages with guest bios and links. These are genuine editorial mentions — Google treats them as natural backlinks. The link compounds: a single appearance generates links that persist for years as the episode page stays online. Additionally, podcast appearances build "entity signals" — Google sees your brand discussed across audio and web, reinforcing your authority. Blue Tree Digital calls it "one of the most underused, ROI-rich methods in digital marketing today."

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Build Your Podcast Target List (2 hours)
1. Search Apple Podcasts, Spotify, and Listen Notes for podcasts in your niche
2. Use competitor data to check if competitors have podcast backlinks (replicate)
3. Target: 20-30 podcasts, mix of small (100-1K listeners) and medium (1K-10K)
4. Check their show notes format — do they include guest links? (Most do)

### Step 2: Create Your Podcast Pitch (1 hour)
- 3-5 unique topic ideas you can discuss with authority
- Your credentials and why their audience would benefit
- Links to any previous podcast appearances or speaking
- Keep pitch under 200 words

### Step 3: Outreach (2-3 hours)
- Personalize each pitch referencing specific episodes you've listened to
- Email, Twitter DM, or podcast booking platforms (PodcastGuests.com, Podmatch)
- Start with smaller podcasts to build a track record, then scale up

### Step 4: Prepare and Deliver
- Research the podcast's style and audience
- Prepare 3-5 talking points with genuine insights
- Mention your website/resource naturally during the conversation
- Provide a dedicated landing page URL (e.g., yoursite.com/podcast) for tracking

### Step 5: Maximize Each Appearance
- Share the episode on your social channels
- Create blog content from the talking points
- Build 1-2 links TO the podcast episode page (amplifies equity to your link)

## Hidden Tips & Tricks

- **Start with NEW/small podcasts**: They're hungry for guests and respond faster. Once you have 3-5 appearances, use those as social proof for bigger shows.
- **The "episode page link" is more valuable than you think**: Podcast episode pages often have internal links from the main podcast site, RSS syndication links, and social shares. One appearance can create a mini link network.
- **Create a dedicated resource page**: Tell listeners "visit [site.com/go]" — this page can serve as a landing page AND collect additional links when people share it.
- **Podcast booking services exist**: Podmatch, PodcastGuests.com, MatchMaker.fm connect guests with hosts. Free to use and dramatically increase your appearance rate.
- **Audio clips → social content → more links**: Clip the best 60 seconds, share as social media content with the podcast tagged. Hosts love this and may link again when resharing.
- **Syndication multiplier**: A single podcast published on Apple, Spotify, Google Podcasts, Stitcher, and the host's website = 3-5 separate link opportunities from one appearance.
- **"Podcast tour" strategy**: Book 10 appearances in 30 days across different podcasts. The burst of mentions and links creates a notable authority signal.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Only targeting the biggest podcasts**: Small podcasts convert faster and still provide quality links
2. **Being too salesy**: Provide value, not a sales pitch. Hosts won't invite you back.
3. **Not requesting the link**: Some hosts forget to add your URL to show notes. Always provide your preferred link after recording.
4. **Inconsistent quality**: One bad appearance can hurt your reputation in the podcast circuit
5. **Not repurposing content**: A podcast appearance is content that can be transcribed, quoted, and shared
